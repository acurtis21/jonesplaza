Design Approach

Theater District Attraction

With increasing residential interest, Downtown is a growing part of Houston. People are seeking a vibrant pedestrian and transit-friendly environment where they have proximity to jobs, amenities and high quality open space. Discovery Green provides a powerful anchor on the east edge of Downtown. Buffalo Bayou is a complementary force on the west edge.

We see the Theater District as a “magnetic field” of culture that generates tremendous energy and buzz for all of Downtown. While it serves a regional audience with its programs, its success as a symbol of creative discovery is an important catalyst for investment and urban life that extends into and beyond the surrounding Historic, Skyline and Civic Center Districts.

Jones Plaza as Epicenter

Within the Theater District, the many venues and performing arts organizations create an intricate constellation surrounding Jones Plaza – orbiting the plaza like planets around the sun, pushing and pulling the plaza with their gravitational forces. As the epicenter of the Theater District, Jones Plaza will be influenced by the proximity of these cultural forces.

An important part of our design process has been understanding the creative strengths of these surrounding institutions and how each can contribute to shaping and programming Jones Plaza. We are also interested in how Jones Plaza can gesture back out to the larger context in terms of visual, physical and programmatic relationships.

People are Performance

If Jones Plaza is the stage, the people of Houston are our players. We envision diverse performances, both programmed and improvised, taking place throughout the year. The pathways and places of Jones Plaza should be understood as experiences first and their physical forms, materials and relationships shaped to stimulate discovery and new encounters.

The elevation of authentic daily rhythms – walking and talking, eating and drinking, singing and dancing – is how we can make this a unique and beloved destination. When you visit, we want you to be moved and to take a sense of relaxation and inspiration with you back out into the world. Jones Plaza will be an oasis in Downtown that contributes to the daily life of Houston’s growing urban population.

Design Urban Choreography

From the Downtown to the district to the human scale, the spaces for urban life at Jones Plaza are shaped by and reciprocally shape flows of urban life. We call this placemaking strategy Urban Choreography.

Inspired by the movements of the performing arts, these gestures connect Jones Plaza to the city and articulate diverse spaces for gathering. These spaces will become stages for creative discovery in partnership with the institutions of the Theater District and the citizens of Houston.

Cultural institutions and Downtown Houston destinations are connected visually, physically and programmatically with Jones Plaza.
Giving built form to invisible forces, Jones Plaza pathways invite passersby into iconic spaces for gathering and performance.

Design Context

Open Space

Jones Plaza contributes to the envisioned ring of green space as a key access point from Downtown to Buffalo Bayou.

Residential Development

Jones Plaza can serve the area’s growing residential population and encourage more people to live, work and play Downtown.

Districts

Jones Plaza is a gateway to the Theater District, but it is also well situated to serve residents and office workers in adjacent neighborhoods.

Design Illustrative Plan

Section (N–S)

The north and south corners of Jones Plaza from Texas Avenue and Capitol Street act as an important first impression and serve as welcoming thresholds.

The top of existing garage structure, requirements for planting depths to support large trees, and retention of significant existing retaining walls were all important considerations in the design process.

Section (W-E)

The west and east corners of Jones Plaza are configured as important areas of the park to inhabit and create spaces that are oriented both towards the surrounding streetscapes and into the park.

A proscenium walk, with multifunctional media towers, frames the Center Green of Jones Plaza, and allows for diverse types of uses, performances, and activities to utilize the space at a variety of scales.

Overview to the North

From the meanders of Buffalo Bayou to the compound curves of Jones Hall, the immediate surroundings inspire and shape Jones Plaza. The Gateway Gardens in the foreground extend an invitation to office workers from the Skyline District and transit riders on the Green Line.

Overview of the South

Approaching from the Westside, visitors will encounter the Spring Stage, with water cascading toward the street in the direction of the bayou. Texas Avenue can be used as a festival street linking Jones Plaza, Fish Plaza and the Bayou.

Street Theater

Jones Plaza can host smaller events in our Street Theater on the east corner. A sidewalk stage allows performers to address an audience in the wooden hillside grandstand or to face the street during street festivals.

Center Green

The Center Green allows large gatherings and events such as simulcast performances from nearby theaters. These could be ticketed and secured or open to all. A graceful ring of trees, sculptural lighting and outside dining frame this flexible open space.

Bayou Bluff

The landscape peels up at the west corner to reveal a restaurant that addresses both the park and the street. This seamless integration of site and structure keeps the attention on the park experience while attracting visitors for dining or drinks in the District.

The Restaurant: Porch & Perch

200 indoor and 80 outdoor seats are provided on two levels with a grand stair and elevator connecting the park through the restaurant to Capitol Street. The flexible dining areas can easily be divided and rented. Accommodation is made for different types of service all day, every day: coffee, grab and go, cafe lunch, dining, pre-theater dining and post-theater cocktails.

Challenges Unique Challenges: Activation & Programming

The most important impact Jones Plaza can have is getting more people to want to live, work and play Downtown. This will stimulate density; increase walking, cycling and transit use; improve the community’s health and wellbeing; and minimize greenhouse gas emissions. The improvements described in this proposal can set the stage for activities and events which attract this urban living. An equally significant dimension, which will need to be developed hand in glove with the physical design, is the activation and programming of this space.

Jones Plaza is fortunate to be able to draw on the creative capacities of the surrounding Theater District institutions, and we would look to coordinate and augment their participation with a series of workshops facilitated by Community Arts Resources. A cyclical rhythm of activities, events and programs will help to bring the plaza to life on a daily, weekly, monthly and annual basis.
